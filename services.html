CSR and Community Engagement Services

Too often, the activities of well-intentioned agriculture and extractives companies are disrupted in Africa because relations with host communities have broken down.

The reasons for this vary, but generally include a disengaged stakeholder strategy, over-reliance on local elite brokers, and a perception that the company in question isn’t being transparent with the communities. Relationship break-downs of this nature have commercial consequences – and can carry significant reputational risk. Establishing a good relationship with local communities, facilitated by well-designed development programmes and effective stakeholder communication, can improve the performance of large-scale agriculture and extractives operations. With over 20 years’ experience working in frontier markets, and more than five years’ experience working with local communities in Sierra Leone, Frontier Research has the expertise to help companies manage these risks.

We do this by offering:

  • Quantitative data collection
  • Focus group discussions & participatory research
  • Mixed-method evaluations
  • Data analysis
